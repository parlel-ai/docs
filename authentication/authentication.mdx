## Overview

Parlel uses a progressive authentication model. Customers start anonymous and gain verified identity as the conversation requires it. Friction scales to sensitivity — a customer asking a general question isn't asked to verify their identity; a customer requesting a subscription change is.

Customers are never over-prompted. They verify when it matters, not as a gate to every interaction.

## How it works

Authentication happens in tiers. Each tier represents a level of verified identity. Actions and journey stages specify the minimum tier required — Parlel enforces this automatically and prompts the customer to verify if they haven't reached the required tier.

The customer's tier persists across the conversation. Once a customer reaches Tier 2, they don't need to verify again for subsequent actions in the same session.

## Authentication tiers

| Tier | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 0 | Anonymous | No identification. General knowledge questions only. |
| Tier 1 | Identified | Customer has provided their email. Parlel has looked them up in your CRM and loaded their account data. |
| Tier 2 | OTP verified | Customer has verified identity via a one-time code sent to their email. Sensitive actions are now available. |
| Tier 3 | Strongly verified | Reserved for future use — BankID and equivalent strong verification. |

## The verification flow

When a customer requests an action that requires a higher tier than they currently hold:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identification prompt">
    The AI asks the customer for their email address. Parlel looks the customer up in your CRM and loads their account data into the session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="OTP verification">
    For Tier 2 actions, Parlel sends a six-digit code to the customer's email. The customer provides the code in the conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Session upgraded">
    Once verified, the session tier upgrades. The customer can proceed with the action without re-verifying for the rest of the session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configuration

### Setting action authentication requirements

Authentication tier requirements are set per action in **Actions** in the portal. As a guideline:
- Read-only lookups — Tier 1
- Any change to customer data — Tier 2
- Financial transactions — Tier 2 minimum

### OTP configuration

OTP codes are sent via your configured email domain. Codes expire after 10 minutes and are invalidated after three failed attempts. Configure your sending domain in **Settings → Email**.
